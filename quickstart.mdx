This guide takes you from a fresh PlanMyRounds account to your first published roster. Plan on about **10 minutes**.

## Prerequisites

- A PlanMyRounds account ([sign up](https://planmyrounds.com/))
- Admin access to your organisation
- A list of staff (names \+ email addresses) you want to roster
- A rough idea of your shift pattern (e.g. _Day SHO 08:00–20:00, Night Reg 20:00–08:00_)

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up your organisation">
    From the dashboard, go to **Organisation Settings** and confirm:

    - Organisation name and time zone
    - Working week (Mon–Sun) and default working hours
    - Minimum rest hours between shifts
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite your staff">
    Go to **User Management → Invite users** and add staff by email. Assign each one a **role** (e.g. SHO, Registrar, Consultant) — roles control which shifts they're eligible for.

    <Tip>
      Bulk-invite via CSV if you have more than 10 users.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your shift templates">
    Go to **Organisation Settings → Shift templates** and add the shifts your team runs regularly.

    For each template, set:

    - Code and name (e.g. `D-SHO`, _Day SHO_)
    - Start and end times
    - Required role
    - Location and break

    See [Shift templates](/shifts/shift-templates) for full details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build your first roster">
    Go to **Rosters → New roster**. Pick a date range (start with one week) and the team it covers.

    In the roster grid, drag templates from the right-hand panel onto the person/date cells. Use **Auto-assign** to fill multiple shifts at once.

    See [Creating shifts](/shifts/creating-shifts) and [Assigning staff](/shifts/assigning-staff).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and publish">
    Click **Review** to check for unassigned shifts, conflicts, and rest-rule breaches. Resolve issues, then click **Publish**.

    Staff are notified by email and in-app. See [Publishing and notifications](/shifts/publishing-and-notifications).
  </Step>
</Steps>
